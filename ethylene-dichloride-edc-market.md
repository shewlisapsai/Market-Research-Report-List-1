<p><strong>The Ethylene Dichloride (EDC) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Direct Chlorination</li><li>Oxychlorination</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Ethylene Dichloride (EDC) is primarily produced through two methods: direct chlorination and oxychlorination. </p><p>In direct chlorination, ethylene reacts with chlorine to form EDC, typically in the presence of a catalyst, resulting in a high yield but requiring careful handling of materials due to toxicity. </p><p>Oxychlorination, on the other hand, involves the reaction of ethylene, hydrogen chloride, and oxygen, producing EDC with lower environmental impact and energy consumption. Both processes are essential for meeting the global demand for EDC in various industrial applications.</p></p>
