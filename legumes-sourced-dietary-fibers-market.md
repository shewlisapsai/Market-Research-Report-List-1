<p><p>The legumes-sourced dietary fibers market is divided into two main types: soluble and insoluble dietary fibers. Soluble dietary fiber, found in legumes like beans and lentils, dissolves in water, forming a gel-like substance that can help regulate blood sugar levels and lower cholesterol. Insoluble dietary fiber, on the other hand, does not dissolve in water and aids in promoting digestive health by adding bulk to the stool, thus preventing constipation. Both types contribute significantly to overall health and wellness.</p></p>
